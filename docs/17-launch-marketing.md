# TrustOS — Launch & Marketing Plan

> GTM for an invitation-only trust network. The core insight: **the product's own mechanics (invites-as-vouches, verified ROI receipts, community leaderboards) are the marketing engine** — paid channels only prime the pump. Conforms to the PRD rollout (India-first, Pune + Bengaluru alpha, per-city liquidity gates) and [`15-opportunity-network-strategy.md`](15-opportunity-network-strategy.md) (community wedge). Assets already built: landing page with explainer video, in-app invite messages, waitlist→vouch loop.

---

## 1. Positioning & message house

**Category:** AI Relationship Intelligence — deliberately *not* "social network," "CRM," or "referral app" (each drags wrong comparisons).
**Tagline:** *Your network is your net worth.*
**One-liner:** The invitation-only network where trusted referrals become verified income.

| Pillar | Message | Proof point |
|---|---|---|
| Money | "Warm intros pay. Verifiably." | Escrowed commissions, ledger settlement, ROI receipt |
| Trust | "A reputation score you can actually explain" | Public Trust Index Policy, factor-level explanations, appeals |
| Effortless | "Your network, working while you sleep" | AI matching, drafted intros, 3-action daily loop |
| Exclusive | "Chosen, not acquired" | Invitation-as-vouch, 5 per member, waitlist routed to members |

**Anti-positioning (say what we're not):** not followers/likes (vs LinkedIn), not link-blasting (vs affiliate), not breakfast meetings and paper slips (vs referral clubs — *respectfully*: early adopters ARE club members; the pitch to them is "keep your chapter, lose the admin," never "your club is obsolete").

**Voice:** calm, premium, specific numbers, zero hustle-bro energy. The brand whispers; the ledger shouts.

## 2. Launch architecture — scarcity as strategy

Three concentric rings, each gated by metrics, not dates:

**Ring 0 — Founding 100 (Pune + Bengaluru, ~6 weeks).** Hand-picked: 40 members of existing referral chapters, 30 SMB owners with active referral behavior (CAs, agencies, clinics-adjacent services, manufacturers), 20 well-networked "super-connectors," 10 businesses to seed campaigns. Everyone onboarded personally (founder does the first 50 — the feedback is the point). Goal: prove aha-in-3-minutes and get the first ₹1L of *settled* commissions — the number the entire story runs on.
**Gate to Ring 1:** ≥60% weekly activation of Founding 100, ≥25 settled outcomes, NPS ≥ 50.

**Ring 1 — Invitation waves (2 cities, months 2–4).** Each Founding member's 5 invites go live → theoretical 500; replenishment through contribution paces further growth. Waitlist opens via the landing page; requests route to members (built). Weekly *invite waves* create rhythm and urgency — "invitations replenished" is a push notification with near-100% open rate.
**Gate to Ring 2:** per-city liquidity (PRD §9: ≥50 active campaigns AND ≥500 monthly referral submissions) OR 90 days, whichever later.

**Ring 2 — City launches (Mumbai, Delhi NCR, Hyderabad, Chennai, months 4–12).** Each city launches with a **Founding Circle** pre-recruited (the Ring-0 playbook repeated by a city lead), a seeded campaign book (10+ businesses committed pre-launch), and a launch dinner (§5). National waitlist counter public on the landing page.

## 3. The wedge playbook — whole communities, not individuals

The highest-leverage acquisition is a **chapter/community lift**: one deal brings 25–40 pre-trusted members plus their existing referral behavior.

- **Target list:** referral-club chapters (members often maintain multiple affiliations), industry associations (CII/MSME chapter subgroups), alumni business circles, co-working communities, founder WhatsApp groups that already pass referrals manually.
- **The host pitch:** "Your community's referral exchange, with a ledger, an AI doing the admin, and revenue share on settled value." (Community revenue share replaces franchise economics — the host gets paid when members win.)
- **Onboarding kit:** white-glove import of the member list as mutual invitations (everyone arrives pre-vouched within the community), a seeded community leaderboard, host training on the Community Health Index, and a "first 30 days" ritual calendar (weekly digest, one challenge, one virtual demo-day).
- **Do NOT poach aggressively from any single organization** — the durable path is members bringing their own chapters, not TrustOS raiding rosters.

## 4. Channels (in priority order)

1. **The invite loop (owned, primary).** Product-native: scarce invites, personal messages, vouch stakes. Instrument K-factor (invites sent × acceptance × activation); target K ≥ 0.7 in Ring 1. Marketing's job is giving members *reasons to spend invites* (invite waves, "your invitee earned their first commission" celebrations).
2. **Founder-led content (LinkedIn + WhatsApp).** 3 posts/week: build-in-public metrics ("₹4.2L settled through the network this month"), trust-score transparency essays, referral-economics teardowns. WhatsApp Status/broadcast for the Indian SMB audience — where they actually are.
3. **The ROI receipt as content.** Monthly, each member gets a shareable receipt card ("TrustOS routed me ₹38,000 in verified business this quarter — [band ring visual]"). Opt-in share to LinkedIn/WhatsApp. This is the testimonial machine: specific, verified, self-interested to share.
4. **Community partnerships & events (§5).**
5. **PR (earned).** Three storylines, in order: (a) *"The anti-social network: a startup that pays you for trust, not attention"* (launch); (b) *the transparent reputation score* — publish the Trust Index Policy and pitch the "algorithmic reputation done right" angle to tech/policy press (differentiates from social-credit fears; our appeals process is the story); (c) *economic outcomes* — "members earned ₹X crore in verified referral income" (month 9+, when the number is real).
6. **Micro-creators (B2B finance/SMB niches, month 4+).** Paid via campaigns on the platform itself (they refer, earn, and document it — with ASCI-compliant disclosure baked into the brief). No generic influencer spend.
7. **Performance ads: minimal and late.** Only to feed the waitlist in launch cities (waitlist CAC target < ₹150); never "download now" — the gate IS the funnel.

## 5. Events — the ceremony layer

- **City launch dinners (30 seats, invitation-printed-on-card energy).** Half members, half waitlist stars. One live demo: submit a real referral on stage, show the escrow ledger entry. Gold-and-navy everything; the brand is the venue.
- **Monthly "Settlement Nights" per city** once liquidity exists: celebrate the quarter's settled outcomes (the only numbers that count), 60 seconds each from top percentile-band members on *how*. This is BNI's recognition ritual, pointed at verified value.
- **Chapter demo mornings** for community-lift prospects: run their existing meeting format *inside* TrustOS once, live.

## 6. Launch sequence (T-8 weeks → T+90 days)

| When | Action |
|---|---|
| T-8w | Founding 100 list built; 10 seed businesses commit campaigns (hand-negotiated: real budgets, ₹500–₹4,000 rewards); legal pre-launch set executed (16 §11) |
| T-6w | Landing page live with waitlist (built); founder content cadence starts; PR agency briefed on storyline (a) |
| T-4w | Founding 100 onboarding begins, personally; first campaigns live; instrument every funnel step |
| T-2w | First settled commission → the screenshot that anchors all launch content |
| **T-0** | Public "launch": landing page + waitlist counter + PR storyline (a) + founder post with the first settlement receipt. **The app is not publicly downloadable-and-usable — the waitlist is the CTA.** Scarcity is the story |
| T+2w | First invite wave; launch dinner #1 (Pune) |
| T+4w | Publish the Trust Index Policy + PR storyline (b) |
| T+6w | First community lift announced (with the chapter's consent, as *their* story) |
| T+90d | Ring-1 gate review: K-factor, liquidity, WTBI. Green → Mumbai Founding Circle recruitment; red → contract to best module pair per PRD kill-gate discipline |

## 7. Metrics that govern the plan

- **North star:** WTBI (weekly trusted business interactions) and **settled ₹ volume** — marketing reports in the same currency as the product.
- **Funnel:** waitlist→invited (target 25% in launch cities), invited→activated (aha completion ≥70%), activated→first contribution in 14d (≥40%), K-factor ≥0.7, month-3 member retention ≥60%.
- **Channel discipline:** CAC by channel measured against *activated member*, not install; kill any channel whose members don't reach first-contribution parity within 2 cohorts.
- **Guardrails (can veto growth):** spam reports <1.5/1k messages, invite-quality (invitee 30-day activation ≥50% — else tighten replenishment), trust-integrity incidents = 0 tolerance in PR window.

## 8. Budget shape (first 6 months, illustrative)

Founder time + 1 growth lead + 1 community manager as the real spend. Cash: events 35% (dinners/settlement nights are the brand), content/design 20%, PR 20%, micro-creator campaigns 15% (routed through the platform itself), waitlist ads 10%. **No spend on installs, ever** — the gate makes install-marketing incoherent.

## 9. Risk & comms playbook

| Risk | Response ready before launch |
|---|---|
| "Social credit score" backlash | Trust Index Policy public from week 4; appeals process is the counter-story; spokesperson brief: contextual, contestable, can't be bought, prohibited uses |
| Scarcity reads as elitism | Waitlist is visibly fair (routed to members, expires, free); "chosen not acquired" copy stays warm, never snobbish |
| Invite selling / gaming incident | Enforcement is the story: publish the ban stats ("we removed X accounts; vouches mean something here") |
| MLM misreading | One-pager ready: single-level, business-funded, sales-outcome-only (16 §1.3); never use "earn by inviting" in any copy — invitations carry zero money by design |
| A chapter-org conflict (poaching optics) | Members bring their own communities; no targeting of any organization's roster; partnership door explicitly open |
